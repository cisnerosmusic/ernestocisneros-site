NFT

A New Chapter in My Creative Life

1. Context: Entering Web3 During Global Collapse

In 2020, at the peak of the COVID-19 pandemic, I was unemployed, isolated at home, and facing the psychological pressure that many artists felt during that period.

Through a small network of Cuban creators, I learned that a new global art movement was emerging online, one where blockchain technology became a tool for preserving digital artworks and connecting communities across borders.

This discovery shifted my perspective from survival to innovation.

2. The Conceptual Foundation: Blockchain as Infrastructure for Art

Although I had followed cryptocurrencies for years, NFTs presented a different paradigm: the use of decentralized ledgers to establish provenance, uniqueness, and value for digital art.

Key realizations during this phase:

  • Blockchain allows artists to retain ownership of their work.
  • Smart contracts enable sovereign distribution independent of platforms.
  • Global collectors can support creators without intermediaries.
  • Communities form organically across languages, cultures, and time zones.

This wasn't financial speculation; it was a new cultural infrastructure.

3. The Clubhouse Era: Knowledge Transfer at Scale

In 2020–2021, Clubhouse functioned as the core intellectual hub of the NFT ecosystem. It enabled:

  • Real-time exchange of creative methodologies
  • Open discussions on smart contracts and marketplaces
  • Cross-disciplinary learning between musicians, coders, visual artists, and collectors
  • Communal problem-solving during market outages and platform transitions

The humility and generosity within those rooms established the cultural ethos that defined the early NFT movement.

4. Early Minting: The Tezos and Ethereum Ecosystems

My first NFTs were minted on Kalamint, an early Tezos marketplace that no longer exists. These original works survived the platform's disappearance and now live on objkt.com, successors to the historic Hic et Nunc protocol, and artist-controlled smart contracts in Tezos FA2 standard.

Later, I expanded into OpenSea (Ethereum), Foundation (during its invitation-only period), community-curated drops and independent smart contract deployments.

Each ecosystem required learning new standards, metadata formats, fee structures, and collector behaviors.

5. Community Dynamics: Latin American Cohesion in a Global Space

Although English dominated the NFT landscape, a significant number of creators were Latin American. This common ground produced:

  • Multilingual collaboration
  • Culturally specific discussions about scarcity, identity, and censorship
  • Strong long-term support networks that extended into real life
  • A sense of shared resilience rooted in our own histories with economic instability and migration

These bonds became fundamental to my long-term engagement in Web3.

6. Technological Integration: AI, Markets, and Smart Contracts

Parallel to blockchain, the rise of AI image models and large language systems became part of our daily tools. Inside the community, we tracked new generative models, metadata standards, IPFS vs. on-chain storage, contract templates, oracle dependencies, Ethereum gas cycles, Tezos governance upgrades, and the social dynamics of the Twitter → X migration.

Market exposure also brought direct conversation with global traders, leading to a deeper understanding of liquidity cycles, collector psychology, narrative-driven valuation, and long-term provenance strategies.

Learning smart contracts became essential, not only to mint, but to survive platform volatility. I adopted practices such as deploying independent contracts, separating metadata hosting layers, maintaining fallback archives, and avoiding single-point-of-failure marketplaces.

7. Artistic Integration: A New Home for Multidisciplinary Work

After decades in music, the NFT ecosystem allowed me to merge sound, digital art, video, literature, memory, code, and community.

For the first time, all disciplines converged naturally. Web3 became a laboratory, not just a marketplace. It offered autonomy, collaboration, and a sense of purpose during one of the most difficult years for artists worldwide.

8. Today: A Continuously Evolving Ecosystem

Today my NFT work exists across multiple chains and formats, always grounded in technological sovereignty, conceptual clarity, multidisciplinary creation, cross-community connection, and long-term preservation of digital work.

The blockchain remains one of the most meaningful creative spaces I have entered: a place where art, technology, and society meet with unprecedented possibility.

Foundation · 2021 · 0.888 ETH
Collaboration with SPHYNX
Collected by @mentalis420

"The Bride of the Moon" marks my genesis on Foundation, a deeply personal piece created in collaboration with my friend and fellow Cuban artist SPHYNX. Conceived as a dialogue between music and image, the work features a fish tracing a perfect circle, alternating black and white in precise synchrony with the music I composed. This circular motion evokes the cycles of life: return, renewal, repetition, and awakening. It was born as a gift of art, soul, and friendship.

Canto del cello

Foundation · 2022 · 1.02 ETH
Collaboration with ojovivo
Collected by Melange fou → LLuvias Imposibles
Exhibited at Lynn University NFT Museum (2023)

Canto del cello is an ode to life, to the uncertain. Perhaps a prize to the one who finds order in the chaos of the universe. Each particle of this world was formed in the core of some star and life is the result of an order beyond reason. Here the shapes and colors have been formed organically, and that is why the cello sings: something totally natural, far from mathematical algorithms. It is passion and life coming from absolute chaos to show a beauty that goes from galaxies to the smallest atomic formations.

The Veil

Foundation · 2021 · 1.5 ETH
Collaboration with ojovivo
Collected by Blocksandart

"The Veil" examines the ancient and recurring symbolism of the veiled female face. For millennia, women have been veiled, sometimes celebrated, sometimes erased. Whether out of fear, reverence, envy, or control, the veil has appeared across cultures and eras: in weddings, temples, funerals. Always present, always ambiguous. Decoration and punishment. Ornament and silence. In this work, the veil becomes metaphor: a marble tombstone that lasts for generations, a prison of ice and salt, a fabric concealing the locked soul of courageous women.

Mother

Foundation · 1.17 ETH
Collaboration with ojovivo
Collected by Liv

"Mother" is a cry, one that emerges from the deepest layers of the Earth. It is a protest, a warning, and a wound. The music carries a deliberately unsettling tension, mirroring the emotional gravity of the image. "Mother" speaks from the voice of Mother Earth, lamenting the irreversible damage inflicted upon her. It is a reflection on the signs we have been receiving for decades: signals of collapse, ecological grief, and the end of the world as we know it. We have all seen the warnings. We acknowledge them. And yet… we do nothing.

Seguir Viva

Tezos · 2021
Collaboration with Kevin Beovides Casas & Greisi González
Music & Editing by Ernesto Cisneros

"Seguir Viva" is a three-way artistic collaboration born in one of the most intense creative periods of the Cuban digital art community. At its heart lies a raw and luminous poem written by Cuban poet Greisi González, speaking from a place of exhaustion, rebellion, tenderness, and survival. Artist Kevin Beovides Casas created digitally manipulated portraits using ASCII Art, with fragments of the poem encoded into the image itself. The soundtrack was composed specifically for this work, following the pulse of Greisi's words.

Discordant

Tezos · 2021 · 1/1
First NFT minted by Ernesto Cisneros Cino

"Discordant" marks the beginning of my journey into the NFT space. Minted on May 10, 2021, it was the first work I ever placed on-chain, created in a moment of intense vulnerability and artistic tension. The drawing was born minutes before an important concert. I was overwhelmed with nerves, unable to settle my mind. Instead of fighting the anxiety, I let it flow through the lines: chaotic, tangled, spiraling. Every stroke became a way to release tension, a visual echo of the inner noise before stepping onto the stage.

N50 (The Betrayal of the Cycle)

Tezos · 2021 · 10 editions

"N50 (The Betrayal of the Cycle)" is a digital artwork built entirely from a single mathematical pattern, an architectural sequence that repeats, reflects, and folds onto itself following the logic of geometric order. Yet at the center, something breaks. Two circles, the only elements not derived from the core pattern, introduce a deliberate asymmetry: a betrayal of the perfect cycle. The work explores the tension between determinism and disruption, between the flawless precision of mathematical systems and the inevitable human impulse that shatters symmetry.
