Some music is not made to be heard. It is made to be remembered. Glacial Paths is an instrumental album for piano and synthesizers; ten pieces composed in the space between silence and sound, between presence and absence. The piano, processed until it breathes like ice, moves slowly through landscapes that have no name but feel familiar: the kind of places we return to when we need to be alone with something we cannot say.

This is not background music. It is companionship for the moments when words fail, for introspection, for the quiet of looking at the sea, for grief that has learned to sit still.

Ten paths. Each one a different way of arriving at the same place.

For Leonor.
