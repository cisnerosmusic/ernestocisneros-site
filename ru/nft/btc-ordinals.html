Ordinal Inscription #95908

This inscription marks a personal and historical milestone.

Ordinal #95908 was created during the earliest exploratory phase of Bitcoin Ordinals, before conventions, large-scale collections, or established narratives existed. At that moment, inscribing on Bitcoin was not yet a standardized practice, but an open question: could Bitcoin serve not only as a ledger of value, but also as a medium for cultural inscription?

This piece is a fragment of an artwork that previously existed on the Tezos blockchain. Its inscription on Bitcoin represents neither duplication nor migration for commercial purposes, but a deliberate act of continuity across chains. It connects two moments of cryptoart history: the experimental, community-driven culture of Tezos and the emergence of Bitcoin as a space for long-term inscription.

Being part of the sub-100k inscriptions situates this Ordinal within a pre-canonical period, when creators were not following rules, but actively defining them. In that sense, its relevance lies less in its appearance and more in its timing.

This inscription is not for sale. Its value is not transactional, but historical and personal. It functions as a trace: a record of presence, authorship, and participation at the moment when Bitcoin first began to be explored as a cultural archive.
